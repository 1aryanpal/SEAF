SEAF

Social Engineering Attack Framework

Documentation

Introduction

The Social Engineering Attack Framework (SEAF) is a research-oriented framework designed to study, model, and analyze social engineering attack techniques in a structured and ethical manner.

SEAF focuses on understanding human-centric attack vectors rather than exploiting them, enabling researchers and defenders to improve awareness, detection, and mitigation strategies.

Objectives

  • Provide a structured taxonomy of social engineering techniques
  • Support academic research and cybersecurity education
  • Enable ethical modeling of attack scenarios
  • Improve defensive awareness and training methodologies

Framework Scope

SEAF is strictly limited to conceptual modeling, simulation, and analysis. It does not provide operational tooling for real-world exploitation.

  • Attack lifecycle modeling
  • Human behavior analysis
  • Threat categorization
  • Defensive mapping and mitigation strategies

Intended Audience

  • Cybersecurity researchers
  • Academic institutions and students
  • Security awareness trainers
  • Authorized security professionals

Ethical & Legal Use

SEAF must only be used in environments where explicit authorization has been granted. Any attempt to use the framework for malicious, deceptive, or unauthorized activities is strictly prohibited.

SEAF is designed for defense, research, and education — not exploitation.

Methodology Overview

The framework follows a phased approach to analyzing social engineering scenarios:

  • Reconnaissance & information gathering (theoretical)
  • Psychological vector identification
  • Attack path modeling
  • Impact assessment
  • Mitigation and awareness mapping

Updates & Versioning

SEAF documentation and structure may evolve over time as research progresses. Updates are published to improve clarity, accuracy, and ethical safeguards.
